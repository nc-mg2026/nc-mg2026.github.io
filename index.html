The 1st National Conference on Mathematical Geometry

Advancing research in mathematical geometry and promoting collaboration between theoretical and applied disciplines.

Strengthening national research in mathematical geometry is essential to promote scientific excellence, innovation, and collaboration between theoretical and applied disciplines. The objectives of MG-2026 are centered on advancing the field of mathematical geometry through: Showcase recent advances in Riemannian structures, differential geometry, analysis, and algebra on manifolds; Explore innovative applications of artificial intelligence in mathematical research; Provide a forum for researchers to exchange knowledge and best practices; Encourage interdisciplinary collaboration across geometry, analysis, algebra, and computational sciences; Identify current challenges and emerging research directions in modern geometry. The program will include keynote lectures, research presentations, thematic sessions, and scientific discussions designed to promote high-level academic exchange and collaboration. MG-2026 is open to undergraduate, Master’s, and PhD students, academic researchers, research laboratories, and specialists working in geometry, analysis, algebra, and AI-driven mathematical modeling.

April 15-16, 2026 (Hybrid : Onsite and Online)
University of Mustapha Stambouli, Mascara

CFP

Conference Objectives

MG-2026 seeks to:

  • Showcase recent advances in Riemannian structures, differential geometry, analysis, and algebra on manifolds.
  • Explore innovative applications of Artificial Intelligence in mathematical research.
  • Encourage interdisciplinary collaboration across geometry, analysis, algebra, and computational sciences.
  • Identify emerging research directions and current challenges in modern geometry.
  • Provide a forum for researchers, students, and laboratories to exchange knowledge and best practices.

The scientific program will include keynote lectures, oral presentations, and thematic sessions.

Conference Topics

Submissions are invited in (but not limited to) the following areas:

I. Riemannian Structures
  • Riemannian manifolds and curvature theory
  • Geodesics and connections
  • Global Riemannian geometry
  • Geometric structures in mathematical physics
II. Differential Geometry
  • Local and global theory of differentiable manifolds
  • Submanifold theory
  • Geometric flows
  • Symplectic and contact geometry
III. Applications of Artificial Intelligence in Mathematics
  • AI-assisted theorem proving
  • Machine learning in geometric analysis
  • Computational geometry and intelligent modeling
  • Data-driven mathematical discovery
IV. Analysis on Differentiable Manifolds
  • Differential operators
  • Partial differential equations
  • Harmonic and spectral analysis
  • Functional analysis in geometric contexts
V. Algebra on Differentiable Manifolds
  • Lie groups and Lie algebras
  • Cohomology theories
  • Fiber bundles
  • Algebraic structures interacting with geometry

Submission Guidelines

All submissions must comply with the following guidelines:

  • Abstract length: 250–300 words
  • Language: English or Arabic
  • Format:
    • Title: Bold, 14 pt, Times New Roman
    • Text: 12 pt, Times New Roman, 1.5 line spacing
  • Authors: Maximum 5
  • Include 5–6 keywords
  • Indicate the corresponding theme (I–V)
  • Provide full institutional affiliation and email of the principal author

Keynote Speakers

Geometry of submanifold

Prof. Mohamed Belkhelfa is a Professor at the Faculty of Exact Sciences at University of Mustapha Stambouli Mascara. He earned his PhD in Mathematics from KU Leuven, specializing in Differential Geometry. His research focuses on the geometry of submanifolds, manifold structures (including contact, Sasaki, Kähler, and Kenmotsu structures), pseudo-symmetry in the sense of R. Deszcz, Thurston geometry, and manifolds with density.

Prof. Belkhelfa has supervised 16 Master’s theses and 13 doctoral dissertations, served as an external examiner for numerous candidates, and published 41 research articles along with 10 refereed conference papers. He has participated in over 50 scientific events, led five research projects, serves as a journal referee and editorial board member of the Tensor Society, and has conducted research visits to more than 25 universities worldwide.

Pr. Mohamed Belkhelfa

University of Mascara

Les applications semi-conformes et le changement triconforme des métriques
